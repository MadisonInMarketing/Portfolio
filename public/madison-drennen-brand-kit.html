Visual Identity System 2026
01 Logo & Wordmark
Madison Drennen · Brand Kit

The mark.

The MD. wordmark is the primary brand identifier. Two letters. One mark. Use it with intention.

ALWAYS

Maintain clear space

Leave breathing room equal to the cap-height of the M on all sides. The mark needs space to be seen.

ALWAYS

Use approved colour combinations

Ink on Cream · Cream on Ink · Cream on Accent Pink. These are the locked combinations.

NEVER

Distort, rotate, or restyle

Don't stretch, skew, add drop shadows, gradients, outlines, or any effects. The mark as designed is the mark.

NEVER

Place on busy backgrounds

Never place the wordmark on photos, patterns, or dark gradients. It disappears -- and so does your credibility.

Two letters. One mark. Used with intention, always.

The 80 / 15 / 5 Rule

Cream and Ink carry 80% of every surface. Accent Pink handles the remaining 15% for CTAs and highlights. Never use it for body text.

Accent Pink is a spark, not a fire

One or two moments per page. A button, a label, an underline. If everything is pink, nothing is pink.

04 Voice & Tone
Madison Drennen · Brand Kit

Sound like a
trusted friend.

Direct, warm, confident. Never corporate. Never salesy. Write like you're talking to someone -- not presenting to a boardroom.

Be direct. Cut the fluff.

Say what you mean in as few words as possible.
DO"We build websites that convert."
DON'T"We leverage our extensive expertise to craft exceptional digital experiences."

Confident, not arrogant.

Own your work without overselling it. Let results speak.
DO"Here's what we built. Here's what happened."
DON'T"We are the premier brand designers in the industry."

Warm but professional.

Write like you're talking to someone -- not presenting to a boardroom.
DO"Not sure which package fits? Let's figure it out."
DON'T"Please do not hesitate to contact us for further information."

Use italics & em dashes.

They add rhythm and personality without exclamation marks.
DO"Everything works together -- website, kit, signature -- as one."
DON'T"Everything works together! We do websites, kits AND signatures!!"

Never apologise for your pricing.

State it clearly. Good work costs money -- and it's worth it.
DO"Starting at $297. Custom quote after your discovery call."
DON'T"Prices vary but we try to be as affordable as possible."
"

The brand isn't the logo. It's how it shows up — every page, every email, every impression.

Consistency is the whole point.

06 AI Prompt Library
Madison Drennen · Brand Kit

Ready-to-paste prompts.

Copy and paste into ChatGPT or Claude. Pre-loaded with your brand voice.

INSTAGRAM CAPTION

Write an Instagram caption for my brand and web design business. I help small businesses look established with websites, brand kits, and email signatures. Tone: direct, confident, warm. No hashtags. End with a subtle CTA. Max 3 sentences.

WEBSITE HERO COPY

Write a hero headline and subheadline for my brand and web design website. Services: websites, brand kits, email signatures. Tone: confident, editorial, minimal. Headline: 4–7 words. Subheadline: 1–2 sentences, no fluff.

COLD OUTREACH EMAIL

Write a cold outreach email to a small business owner with a dated website. I'm a brand and web designer. Under 80 words. Tone: friendly, direct, not pushy. Lead with what's in it for them. One soft CTA -- a free discovery call.

PACKAGE DESCRIPTION

Write a 2–3 sentence description of my brand kit package. It includes: color palette, typography system, logo usage rules, voice & tone guide, and an AI prompt library. Tone: confident, clear, no jargon. Speak to a small business owner.

LINKEDIN POST

Write a LinkedIn post about why small businesses need a brand kit PDF. I offer brand kits as a service. Tone: educational, confident, not preachy. Short paragraphs. Hook in the first line. End with a question. Max 150 words.

EMAIL SUBJECT LINES

Write 5 email subject lines for a newsletter from a brand and web designer. Audience: small business owners. Goal: get them to think about their brand. Tone: direct, intriguing. Each subject line under 8 words.

TESTIMONIAL REQUEST

Write a short message asking a past client to leave a Google review. I designed their website and brand kit. Tone: warm, brief, no pressure. Give them 2–3 optional prompts to answer. Under 60 words.

FAQ ANSWER

Write an FAQ answer to: "How long does it take to build a website?" I'm a brand and web designer for small businesses. Timeline: 2–4 weeks. Tone: clear, honest, confident. 2–3 sentences max. No corporate fluff.

Pre-loaded with your brand voice —— copy, paste, and ship.
